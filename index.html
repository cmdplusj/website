But wait... what is ‘Mentorship’?


Broadly speaking, mentorship can be defined as advising or helping a less-experienced person, so that they make progress towards their goals.


You know that advice your college senior gave you for getting an intern? That was mentorship.


Or when a friend made you realise you were being underpaid and should switch roles? That too.


Or your elder sister helping you figure out your 5 year career plan? Yes! Even that!

How can a mentor help you?

A great mentor can help clarify long-term goals, develop plans for advancing your career, or even train you on soft skills. There’s so much to learn.

FAQs

Have a question and don’t see it here? Reach out to us

Is this 100% Free?
Command+J a completely free and open to all! The only cost here is some enthusiasm from your side and following our code of conduct.
What if I don’t want to get on a video call?
We highly recommend using a video call to talk to each other for the first time. It helps build a bond and is efficient. On the day of the call, we'll be CCing you and the mentor, in an email thread where y'all can discuss the mode of communication that works for both of you.
How do I stay in touch with my mentor?
Let's consider 2 scenarios -
1. You could have your first call with your mentor and decide on a path forward together through regularly scheduled calls.
2. You could reach out to each other as and when you want to - to catch up or otherwise.
Of course, not all mentor/mentee relationships need to last long. Sometimes it’s okay to just talk once if there’s no need for continuity.
What if I didn’t find a mentor in the same field as me?
Even if there's a mismatch in hard skills or career trajectory, you can still learn a lot from a mentor. We encourage all mentees to read between the lines while reading the bios of mentors for the same reason! Sometimes, you find common ground in similar experiences.
